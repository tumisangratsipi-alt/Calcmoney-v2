Society places almost total emphasis on salary. When someone is referred to as "rich," it almost exclusively means they have a high-income W-2 job—a physician making $300,000 or a software engineer earning $250,000. 

But a high salary does not equal wealth. Salary is a flow of water pouring into a bucket. If that bucket has massive holes in the bottom (expensive car leases, $800 dining tabs, bloated mortgages), the bucket will remain empty regardless of how fast the water is pouring in.

**Net Worth** is the water remaining in the bucket at the end of the day. It is the absolute, unmanipulable scoreboard of your financial reality.

## The Tragedy of "Looking Wealthy"

Consider two individuals living next door to each other:

*   **Neighbor A:** A corporate lawyer earning $250,000 a year. He drives a brand new leased Porsche 911, lives in a sprawling $1.2M home with a $1.1M mortgage, carries $200k in law school debt, and has $10,000 in a checking account. Because his debts far exceed his liquid cash and home equity, his Net Worth is **Negative -$100,000**. He is technically poorer than a college student with zero dollars and zero debt. 
*   **Neighbor B:** A high school vice principal earning $75,000 a year. She drives a paid-off 2012 Honda Civic, aggressively pays down her modest $200k mortgage, and maxes out her 401(k) every single year. Her home equity and Vanguard index funds total $450,000. Her only debt is the remaining $50k on the mortgage. Her Net Worth is a robust **$400,000**. 

Neighbor A looks rich, but is broke and highly vulnerable to a single layoff. Neighbor B looks incredibly average, but is fundamentally wealthy and securely autonomous.

## The Simple Equation: Assets Minus Liabilities

Calculating your Net Worth is a sobering exercise. You must meticulously catalog everything.

### 1. Catalog Your Assets (What You Own)
*   **Liquid Assets:** Checking accounts, High Yield Savings, physical cash, emergency funds.
*   **Investment Assets:** 401(k) balances, Roth IRAs, taxable brokerage accounts (stocks, crypto), Health Savings Accounts (HSA).
*   **Hard Assets:** The conservative appraisal value of your real estate, Kelly Blue Book trade-in value of vehicles, and verified precious metals. Do not include your TV, couch, or clothing. 

### 2. Catalog Your Liabilities (What You Owe)
*   **Real Estate Debt:** The exact remaining principal payload on your primary mortgage and any HELOCs.
*   **Vehicle Debt:** Remaining principal on auto loans.
*   **Education Debt:** Student loans (federal and private).
*   **Toxic Debt:** Outstanding credit card balances and high-rate personal loans.

### 3. The Final Execution
Simply subtract the Liabilities from the Assets. That absolute number represents your exact financial footprint on Earth.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Is it normal to have a negative Net Worth in your 20s?**
Absolutely. If you graduated with $80,000 in student loans and have had zero years in the workforce to accumulate assets, you begin the game at -80,000. The objective in your 20s is simply crossing the "Zero" threshold into positive territory by ruthlessly destroying that debt.

**If I own a business, do I include it?**
Yes, but business valuation is highly subjective. A general rule of thumb for small service businesses is to multiply your Net Annual Profit by 2 or 3. If your plumbing business profits $100k a year, it adds roughly $250,000 to your asset column.
